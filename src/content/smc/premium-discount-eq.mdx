# Premium / Discount / Equilibrium

<ConceptCard title="Premium / Discount / Equilibrium" icon="⚖️">
  Every price swing can be divided into **value zones** using its range. The **equilibrium** (50% level) splits the swing into two halves:

  - **Premium zone** (above equilibrium): Price is relatively **expensive**. In a bearish context, smart money looks to sell here — distributing positions to willing buyers who perceive the move as continuation.
  - **Discount zone** (below equilibrium): Price is relatively **cheap**. In a bullish context, smart money looks to buy here — accumulating positions from panicked sellers.

  This framework ensures you are always asking: *"Am I buying at a discount or selling at a premium?"* — the foundational logic behind institutional positioning.
</ConceptCard>

## Mapping the Zones

<AnnotatedChart title="Premium, Discount & Equilibrium Zones" showSwingHL={true} />

To apply this framework to any chart:

1. **Identify the relevant swing** — the most recent significant high-to-low (bearish leg) or low-to-high (bullish leg) on your analysis timeframe.
2. **Mark the 50% level (equilibrium)** — this is the midpoint of the swing. Many traders use a Fibonacci retracement tool, where 0.5 corresponds to equilibrium.
3. **Define the premium zone** — everything above the 50% level, up to the swing high.
4. **Define the discount zone** — everything below the 50% level, down to the swing low.

Smart money logic is straightforward: **buy below equilibrium, sell above equilibrium.** This aligns entries with institutional positioning rather than chasing extended moves.

## The Optimal Trade Entry (OTE) Zone

Within the premium and discount zones, the **0.618 – 0.786 Fibonacci retracement** range is referred to as the **Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)** zone. This is the area where the highest-probability institutional entries tend to cluster.

- In a bullish retracement (buying the dip), the OTE sits in the **discount** — between the 61.8% and 78.6% retracement levels measured from the swing low.
- In a bearish retracement (selling the rally), the OTE sits in the **premium** — between the 61.8% and 78.6% retracement levels measured from the swing high.

The OTE zone often overlaps with other confluence factors — order blocks, FVGs, or prior structural levels — making it a particularly powerful area for entries.

## Applying Premium/Discount to Bias

The premium/discount framework is not a standalone signal — it is a **filter** for existing trade ideas:

- If your higher-timeframe bias is bullish, look for entries **only in discount** on the lower timeframe. Ignore sell setups in premium.
- If your higher-timeframe bias is bearish, look for entries **only in premium** on the lower timeframe. Ignore buy setups in discount.
- Price trading at **equilibrium** is a neutral zone — neither expensive nor cheap. It is generally not an ideal area to initiate positions.

<Callout type="trap" title="Common Mistake: Applying Premium/Discount to the Wrong Swing">
  The framework only works when applied to the **correct swing** — the one that defines the current dealing range on your analysis timeframe. Using a minor intraday swing on a 5-minute chart while your trade thesis is based on the daily chart will produce misleading zones. Always anchor premium/discount to the swing that matches your trade's timeframe and thesis.
</Callout>
