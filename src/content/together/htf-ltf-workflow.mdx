# HTF → LTF Analysis Workflow

<ConceptCard title="Top-Down Analysis" icon="🔭">
The process of determining the overall market narrative on Higher Timeframes (HTF) and zooming into Lower Timeframes (LTF) for precise trade execution.
</ConceptCard>

## The Workflow

The secret to sniper entries is not a magical LTF indicator; it's entering on the LTF *at the exact moment* the HTF narrative dictates a reaction.

<StepFlow>
  <Step title="1. Daily / 4H (The Narrative)">
    Identify the overarching trend (Market Structure) and the next major draw on liquidity (Key Levels). Are we bullish or bearish? Where is price likely going next?
  </Step>
  <Step title="2. 1H / 15M (The Setup)">
    Wait for price to reach a high-probability HTF Zone (e.g., an unmitigated 4H Order Block or a sweep of a Daily High). Do not execute yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="3. 5M / 1M (The Execution)">
    Once in the HTF Zone, zoom into the LTF. Wait for a structural shift (CHoCH) and strong displacement (FVG) confirming the HTF idea. Enter on the LTF pullback to minimize risk and maximize Reward-to-Risk (RR).
  </Step>
</StepFlow>

<Callout type="trap" title="Common Mistake: Timeframe Confusion">
A common trap is becoming bearish on the 5M chart when the 4H chart is heavily bullish. The 5M downtrend is simply a pullback to a 4H discount zone. Always align your LTF entries with the HTF narrative.
</Callout>

<KeyTakeaways>
- HTF provides the *direction* and *context*.
- LTF provides the *entry* and *risk management*.
- Never trade the LTF in isolation without checking the HTF.
</KeyTakeaways>
