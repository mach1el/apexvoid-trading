# How the Frameworks Relate

<ConceptCard title="The Meta Framework" icon="🧩">
SMC, ICT, Price Action, and Supply & Demand are largely different naming conventions for the same underlying market mechanics: liquidity and order flow.
</ConceptCard>

## The Durable Core

The trading industry is full of marketing. Different educators package the same concepts under new names to sell courses. However, the durable core of all successful charting frameworks relies on:

1. **Market Structure:** Defining the trend (HH/HL or LH/LL).
2. **Key Levels:** Identifying where liquidity rests (Swing points, Session Highs/Lows).
3. **Confluence:** Stacking probabilities.
4. **Risk Management:** The true key to longevity.

<Callout type="note" title="A Neutral Perspective">
We do not advocate for one specific "secret system." Whether you call a zone an "Order Block," a "Supply Zone," or "Institutional Footprint," the chart respects the liquidity, not the label you give it.
</Callout>

### Translation Guide

- **BOS (Break of Structure)** = Trend Continuation
- **CHoCH (Change of Character)** = Trend Reversal / Shift in Momentum
- **Order Block (OB)** = Last point of liquidity injection before displacement
- **FVG (Fair Value Gap)** = Imbalance / Inefficient Price Delivery
- **Liquidity Sweep** = Stop Hunt / False Breakout

<KeyTakeaways>
- Focus on the *mechanics* (why price moves) rather than the *labels*.
- All frameworks share a common lineage.
- Master the core concepts, and you will understand any framework.
</KeyTakeaways>
