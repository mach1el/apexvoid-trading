# Glossary

<Glossary>
  <Term name="BOS" definition="Break of Structure. A continuation of the current trend, closing past a previous structural high (uptrend) or low (downtrend)." />
  <Term name="CHoCH" definition="Change of Character. The first break of structure in the opposite direction of the trend, signaling a potential reversal." />
  <Term name="OB" definition="Order Block. The last opposing candle before a strong displacement move, representing institutional footprints." />
  <Term name="FVG" definition="Fair Value Gap. A three-candle pattern where price moves so rapidly it leaves a gap between the wicks of the 1st and 3rd candle." />
  <Term name="PDH / PDL" definition="Previous Day High / Previous Day Low. Common liquidity targets." />
  <Term name="PWH / PWL" definition="Previous Week High / Previous Week Low. Major higher-timeframe liquidity targets." />
  <Term name="HTF / LTF" definition="Higher Timeframe / Lower Timeframe. HTF provides context; LTF provides execution." />
  <Term name="Swing High" definition="A fractal peak where the middle candle has a higher high than the candles immediately to its left and right." />
  <Term name="Swing Low" definition="A fractal trough where the middle candle has a lower low than the candles immediately to its left and right." />
  <Term name="Liquidity" definition="Clusters of resting orders (usually stop-losses) acting as magnets for price." />
  <Term name="Displacement" definition="A strong, aggressive move in price leaving large bodied candles and often FVGs." />
  <Term name="Imbalance" definition="Another term for Fair Value Gap (FVG) or inefficient price delivery." />
  <Term name="OTE" definition="Optimal Trade Entry. The 62% to 79% retracement level of a Fibonacci draw, considered a high-probability entry zone." />
  <Term name="Killzone" definition="Specific time windows (e.g., London Open, NY Open) characterized by high volatility and institutional manipulation." />
</Glossary>
