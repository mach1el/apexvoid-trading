# Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)

The **Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)** is a specific retracement zone — the **62% to 79% Fibonacci levels** — where ICT methodology suggests the highest-probability entries occur during a pullback within an established trend. The concept combines Fibonacci retracement with structural context to define a "sweet spot" for entries.

---

## The Logic Behind OTE

When price makes an impulsive move (displacement), it often retraces before continuing. The question every trader faces is: *how deep will the retracement go?*

The OTE zone answers this with a probability-weighted framework:

<ConceptCard title="The OTE Zone" icon="🎯">
  <p>Draw a Fibonacci retracement from the <strong>swing low</strong> to the <strong>swing high</strong> (for bullish OTE) or <strong>swing high</strong> to <strong>swing low</strong> (for bearish OTE).</p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>62% retracement</strong> — The top of the OTE zone. Shallow retracements stop here.</li>
    <li><strong>70.5% retracement</strong> — The midpoint, often called the "optimal" level. This is where many ICT practitioners focus.</li>
    <li><strong>79% retracement</strong> — The bottom of the OTE zone. Deep retracements that still maintain trend structure.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Below 79%, the retracement starts to threaten the trend itself. Above 62%, the entry may be too early or too expensive relative to the pullback.</p>
</ConceptCard>

## Applying OTE Step by Step

<StepFlow>
  <Step>
    <strong>Identify the impulse leg.</strong> A clear displacement move with a BOS or CHoCH confirming the direction. This is your swing low to swing high (bullish) or swing high to swing low (bearish).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    <strong>Apply Fibonacci retracement.</strong> Anchor the Fibonacci tool at the origin of the impulse (swing low for bullish) and extend it to the terminus (swing high for bullish).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    <strong>Mark the OTE zone.</strong> Highlight the region between the 62% and 79% retracement levels. This is your watch zone.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    <strong>Wait for price to enter the zone.</strong> Do not enter preemptively. Wait for price to actually retrace into the 62–79% region.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    <strong>Look for a confluence trigger within the zone.</strong> An OB, FVG, or session-level alignment within the OTE zone significantly increases the quality of the setup.
  </Step>
</StepFlow>

## What OTE Is Not

<Callout type="warn" title="Important Distinctions">
  <p>OTE is <strong>not</strong> just "any Fibonacci retracement." The concept requires:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>A <strong>confirmed directional move</strong> (BOS/CHoCH) — not just any swing.</li>
    <li><strong>Structural context</strong> — the retracement must occur within a valid trend, not a choppy range.</li>
    <li><strong>Confluence</strong> — OTE works best when a PD array (OB, FVG) aligns with the fib zone. Naked fib levels alone are weaker.</li>
  </ul>
</Callout>

## OTE vs. General Fibonacci Trading

<CompareTable title="OTE vs. Standard Fibonacci Retracement">

| Aspect | Standard Fib | ICT OTE |
|---|---|---|
| **Levels used** | 38.2%, 50%, 61.8% | 62%, 70.5%, 79% |
| **Context required** | Often applied mechanically | Requires BOS/CHoCH confirmation first |
| **Confluence** | Optional | Essential — must align with PD arrays |
| **Entry trigger** | Touch of level | Reaction at level + structural confirmation |
| **Stop placement** | Below the fib level | Below the swing origin |

</CompareTable>

<KeyTakeaways>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>OTE</strong> is the 62–79% Fibonacci retracement zone within a confirmed impulse move.</li>
    <li>The <strong>70.5% level</strong> is the midpoint and most commonly referenced entry price.</li>
    <li>OTE requires a <strong>prior BOS or CHoCH</strong> — it's not a standalone fib tool.</li>
    <li>Highest-quality OTE setups have a <strong>PD array (OB or FVG) inside the zone</strong>.</li>
    <li>Below 79%, the retracement threatens trend integrity. Above 62%, the pullback may be too shallow for optimal risk:reward.</li>
  </ul>
</KeyTakeaways>
