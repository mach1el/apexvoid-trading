# Judas Swing & Liquidity Run

The **Judas swing** is a deceptive price move that occurs at or near a session open. Price initially moves in one direction — triggering breakout traders and sweeping resting liquidity — before sharply reversing to move in the opposite, "true" direction for the session. The name references the biblical betrayal: the initial move *appears* to be the real move, but it is a trap.

---

## The Anatomy of a Judas Swing

<ConceptCard title="How It Works" icon="🔄">
  <p>At the open of a killzone (typically London or New York), price makes an <strong>initial thrust</strong> in one direction. This thrust does three things:</p>
  <ol>
    <li><strong>Sweeps resting liquidity</strong> — stop losses above a prior high or below a prior low are triggered, filling institutional orders.</li>
    <li><strong>Induces breakout entries</strong> — retail traders see the move and enter in the direction of the thrust, providing counter-liquidity.</li>
    <li><strong>Engineers the reversal</strong> — once liquidity is absorbed, price reverses and those freshly entered positions become fuel for the real move.</li>
  </ol>
</ConceptCard>

## When Judas Swings Occur

<CompareTable title="Typical Judas Swing Scenarios">

| Session | Common Pattern | What Gets Swept |
|---|---|---|
| **London open** | Price sweeps the Asian high or low, then reverses. | Asian session liquidity (equal highs/lows formed overnight). |
| **New York open** | Price sweeps London's session high or low, then reverses. | London session liquidity, especially if London's move was over-extended. |
| **Sunday open** | Price gaps into Friday's range, sweeps a level, then reverses. | Weekend gap liquidity — less reliable, wider spreads. |

</CompareTable>

## Identifying a Judas Swing in Real Time

<StepFlow>
  <Step>
    <strong>Note the prior session's range.</strong> Mark the Asian session high and low (for London) or the London session high and low (for New York). These are the liquidity targets.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    <strong>Watch the initial move at session open.</strong> Does price thrust toward one of these levels? If it sweeps the high or low with momentum, this is a potential Judas swing in progress.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    <strong>Wait for the reversal signal.</strong> Do not pre-empt. Look for a <strong>displacement candle</strong> in the opposite direction, a CHoCH on the lower timeframe, or a break back into the prior range.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    <strong>Confirm with structure.</strong> The reversal should align with the higher-timeframe bias. A Judas swing that sweeps the Asian high and reverses bearish is strongest when the daily or 4H trend is also bearish.
  </Step>
</StepFlow>

<Callout type="trap" title="Common Trap">
  <p>Not every session-open move is a Judas swing. Sometimes the initial thrust <strong>is</strong> the real move, and the "expected reversal" never comes. This is why <strong>confirmation</strong> (CHoCH, displacement, structural shift) is essential before entering against the initial move.</p>
</Callout>

## Judas Swing vs. Liquidity Sweep

The Judas swing is a *specific type* of liquidity sweep — one that occurs at a session open and is followed by a directional commitment for the session. Not all liquidity sweeps are Judas swings:

- **Liquidity sweep** — Any move that takes out resting orders at a key level. Can happen at any time.
- **Judas swing** — A liquidity sweep that occurs *at the open of a killzone*, is *deceptive* (against the true session direction), and is followed by *reversal and displacement*.

<KeyTakeaways>
  <ul>
    <li>A <strong>Judas swing</strong> is a false move at session open that sweeps liquidity before price reverses to the true direction.</li>
    <li>Most common at <strong>London open</strong> (sweeping Asian range) and <strong>New York open</strong> (sweeping London range).</li>
    <li>The sweep triggers stops and induces breakout entries — providing <strong>counter-liquidity</strong> for institutional positioning.</li>
    <li><strong>Never trade the Judas direction.</strong> Wait for the reversal confirmation: CHoCH, displacement, or a structural shift.</li>
    <li>Judas swings are most reliable when they align with the <strong>higher-timeframe directional bias</strong>.</li>
  </ul>
</KeyTakeaways>
