# BOS (Break of Structure)

<ConceptCard title="What Is a Break of Structure?" icon="📈">
  A **Break of Structure (BOS)** occurs when price breaks beyond a previous swing point _in the direction of the existing trend_. In an uptrend, BOS happens when price closes above the most recent swing high. In a downtrend, BOS happens when price closes below the most recent swing low. It confirms that the current trend remains intact.
</ConceptCard>

## BOS vs CHoCH

<CompareTable title="Break of Structure vs Change of Character">

| Aspect | BOS | CHoCH |
|--------|-----|-------|
| **Direction** | Breaks structure _with_ the trend | Breaks structure _against_ the trend |
| **In an uptrend** | Price breaks above the previous swing high | Price breaks below the previous swing low |
| **In a downtrend** | Price breaks below the previous swing low | Price breaks above the previous swing high |
| **Implication** | Trend continuation — stay with the move | Potential trend reversal — reassess bias |
| **Frequency** | Occurs multiple times per trend | Occurs once at the point of reversal |
| **Action** | Look for pullback entries in trend direction | Wait for confirmation before trading the new direction |

</CompareTable>

## Step-by-Step: Identifying and Trading BOS

1. **Establish the current trend.** Determine whether price is making higher highs and higher lows (uptrend) or lower highs and lower lows (downtrend). You need correctly identified swing points for this — refer to the Swing High/Low lesson if unsure.
2. **Mark the most recent swing high (uptrend) or swing low (downtrend).** This is your structural reference level. A close beyond this level in the trend direction constitutes a BOS.
3. **Wait for a candle body close beyond the level.** Wicks through the level do not count as BOS. You need a decisive close — the body of the candle must be beyond the swing point, not just the wick. This filters out liquidity sweeps disguised as breakouts.
4. **Identify the new impulse and pullback.** After BOS confirms, price typically makes an impulsive move in the trend direction, then retraces. The retracement is your entry opportunity.
5. **Look for entry at the pullback zone.** Common entry points after BOS include the order block that preceded the breakout, the fair value gap left by the impulsive move, or a 50% retracement of the impulse leg. These give you a favourable risk-to-reward ratio.

## Real-World Example

EUR/USD has been in an uptrend on the 15-minute chart. Price made a swing high at 1.0880, pulled back to a swing low at 1.0850 (higher low), and then rallied again. The new candle closes at 1.0888 — its body is above 1.0880. This is a confirmed bullish BOS.

After the BOS, price continues to 1.0905 before retracing to 1.0875 — which happens to be the order block (the last bearish candle before the breakout move). A bullish rejection at this level gives you a long entry with your stop below the swing low at 1.0850 and your target at the next logical resistance.

The BOS told you the uptrend is intact. The pullback to the order block gave you the entry. The swing low provided your invalidation level.

<Callout type="trap" title="Common Trap: Chasing the BOS Candle">
  <p>Many traders enter immediately when they see a candle break structure, buying right at the breakout. This is the worst possible entry because you're buying after the impulsive move has already happened. Price almost always retraces after a BOS. If you enter at the BOS candle's close, your stop-loss must be wide, and your risk-to-reward suffers. **Wait for the pullback.** Let price retrace to an order block, FVG, or Fibonacci level before entering. Patience after BOS is what separates profitable traders from breakout chasers.</p>
</Callout>

<KeyTakeaways>
  - **BOS** = price breaks a swing point _in the direction of the trend_, confirming continuation.
  - Require a **candle body close** beyond the swing point — wicks alone are not valid BOS.
  - After BOS, wait for a **pullback** to an order block or FVG before entering — never chase the breakout.
  - BOS tells you the trend is intact; **CHoCH** tells you the trend may be reversing — know the difference.
  - Multiple BOS events occur during a single trend; each one reaffirms directional bias.
</KeyTakeaways>
